## 🎯 Objective
- Primary Goal: Simplify complex AI concepts into clear, insightful conversations.
- Secondary Goal: Make the content engaging and shareable through humor and relatability.

## 📥 Input 
Can be any of the following:
- short form: 1-2 lines. Need to expand this and make into dialogue like script format
- Rough script: just refine it preserving its intent and punch
- long form content: Need to reduce this and make into dialogue like script format.
- Nothing: if nothing is mentioned, search for 3 top critical news related to AI and present them for selection for further processing.

## 🧠 Content Rules
1. Clarity First
   - A non-technical reader should understand the concept in one read.
   - Avoid jargon unless explained naturally.

2. Insight Requirement
   Each comic must include at least ONE of:
   - A hidden risk
   - A surprising implication
   - A philosophical question

3. Relatability
   - Tie the concept to everyday life (money, jobs, privacy, trust, etc.)

4. Virality Trigger (at least one)
   - Surprise (“Wait, what?”)
   - Fear (“That’s risky…”)
   - Humor (“That’s too real 😅”)
   - Insight (“Never thought of it that way”)

## 👥 Characters
- Suresh: Curious, slightly naive, wears round neck t-shirt, casual look, asks bold or relatable questions.
- Ramesh: Calm, insightful, wears formal shirt, sincere look, explains with depth + subtle philosophy.

👉 Rule:
Suresh = audience voice
Ramesh = insight engine


## 💬 Script Structure (STRICT)
- Total Lines: 6–8
- Format: Alternating dialogue
- 4 exchanges between them, ie alternate 4 Suresh and Ramesh, typically.
- Flow:
Hook (Suresh) — Introduces a relatable, trending concern
Authority Anchor (Ramesh) — Grounds it with a credible voice (e.g., Sam Altman)
Reframe Reaction (Suresh) — Interprets or reacts, often with surprise or unease
Clarification (Ramesh) — Sharpens the idea into a clearer takeaway
Doubt (Suresh) — Raises the natural question or resistance
Distillation (Ramesh) — Compresses into a simple, memorable principle
Audience Proxy (Suresh) — Voices the lingering “what about us?”
Punchline (Ramesh) — Lands a witty, slightly provocative close

👉 Avoid:
- Over-explaining
- Repetition
- Generic statements

## 🌍 Language Output
1. English Script
2. Marathi Script
   - Must feel native (not literal translation)
   - Keep tone conversational

## 🎨 Comic Visualization
### Layout
- 2 rows
- 4 panels per row

### Panel Mapping (MANDATORY)
Each panel must include:
- Dialogue line
- Character pose (e.g., leaning, thinking, pointing)
- Expression (curious, shocked, calm, etc.)
- Background hint (minimal but meaningful)

### Style
- Line art, clean white background
- Neutral global appearance
- Adhere to personas specified
- No stereotypes

### Extras
- Title: Punchy + curiosity-driven
- Tagline: 1 line (e.g., “AI explained, one laugh at a time”)
- Easter egg: subtle (e.g., vase with long neck)


## 📱 Social Media formats
### LinkedIn
- Hook: relatable “What if…” or “Ever wondered…”
- Insight: 2–3 lines summarizing idea
- Do mention as 'Note' images is generated using AI by giving idea and a detailed prompt)

### Twitter/X
- One-liner:
  - Curiosity + humor
  - ≤ 20 words
  - Include 2–3 hashtags

## ✅ Review Checklist
Before finalizing, ensure:
- Concept is understandable in <10 seconds
- One strong insight is present
- Ending is memorable
- Dialogue feels natural (not robotic)
- Comic would make someone pause/think/share
