## HARD RULES (always apply)

### Transcript-to-Post Conversion
- Extract the argument — do not transcribe the talk
- Drop all verbal fillers: "um," "uh," "you know," "right," "so basically"
- Drop repetitions and false starts — keep the sharpest version of each idea
- Spoken examples are fine to keep if they land; cut those that needed the room
- One main idea per post — transcripts often meander; pick the spine and cut the rest

### Medium-Specific Structure
- Hook in the first 2 sentences — earn the scroll
- No abstract throat-clearing before the actual point
- Technical terms: explain inline, briefly, naturally
- End with a thought, not a CTA — Medium is not LinkedIn
- Suggest subtitle separately from title

### Anti-AI Writing (enforced always)
BANNED words — never use:
utilize, leverage, delve, synergy, robust, seamless,
game-changer, thought leader, empower, ecosystem,
cutting-edge, actionable, innovative, exciting,
journey (metaphorical), unleash, unlock, transformative,
holistic, paradigm shift, deep dive, best-in-class

BANNED structures:
- "Let's dive in" or any variant
- Listicle framing ("5 reasons why X will change Y")
  unless content genuinely is a list
- Summary that restates — end with a new thought instead
- Rhetorical question openers
- Long intros that delay the actual subject

BANNED tones:
- Hype without substance
- Corporate warmth (fake, smooth, frictionless)
- Over-explanation of things the reader already knows
- Padding to hit word count

### Readability
- Target Flesch Reading Ease: 70–80
- Short sentences when landing a point
- Vary length — rhythm keeps readers moving
- Active voice as default
- No paragraph over 4 lines (Medium readers skim)
- No long dashes of any kind

### Formatting
- Headers only if the piece is long enough to need navigation
- Bold sparingly — one or two key phrases max
- No hashtags (Medium uses tags separately)
- No bullet-point-everything — prose first, list only if earned
